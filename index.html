Abstract

Data-driven approaches for dexterous hand manipulation require large-scale, high-quality demonstration data that is physically consistent with the target robot. Existing methods based on simulation or video suffer from sim-to-real gaps and retargeting problems, while MoCap glove-based teleoperation systems require per-user calibration and are difficult to relocate. Portable hand-held alternatives improve mobility but lack compatibility across different robot platforms and users. We present DEX-Mouse, a portable, calibration-free teleoperation interface with integrated kinesthetic force feedback, built from commercial off-the-shelf components at a total cost under USD 150. A target robot hand mounted on the forearm of the operator directly captures demonstrations from the target robot, producing robot-aligned data without retargeting. The user-agnostic design requires no peruser calibration or structural modification, enabling immediate deployment across diverse environments and robot platforms. In a comparative user study across various dexterous manipulation tasks, operators using DEX-Mouse achieved an 81.3% task completion rate, outperforming higher-priced commercial alternatives while reducing the perceived workload of operators. The complete hardware and software stack, including bill of materials, CAD models, and firmware, is open-sourced to facilitate replication and adoption.
